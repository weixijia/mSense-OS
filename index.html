Why hardware control matters

Most mmWave systems expose processed outputs, not the raw signals AI needs.

Commercial radar today is built on OEM hardware and closed SDKs. Cost, data format, and capability are set by the vendor — and teams are usually left with sparse point clouds or coarse detection, which is why the market stalls at presence, occupancy, and fall detection.

Off-the-shelf · point cloud

A sensor you can't shape

  • Sparse, heavily processed output
  • Micro-motion & Doppler discarded
  • Environment-sensitive, hard to learn from
  • Limited to coarse detection tasks
mSense OS · raw RA / RD / RAD

A system you design

  • Rich motion & spatial information
  • Preserves Doppler and range-angle structure
  • Supports self-supervised learning
  • Enables HAR, pose, captioning, scene understanding

Design for the use case

Because we control the stack, we design around each customer's real constraints.

01

Signal fidelity

Tasks differ. Pose and rehab need fine-grained motion, resolution, and sync; presence detection does not. We tune raw-data access, RA/RD/RAD, frame rate, and synchronization to the job.

02

Deployment environment

Home, clinic, vehicle, outdoor, and lab differ in mounting, occlusion, space, and interference. Control lets us tune radar placement, sensor mix, and calibration per scene.

03

Cost & form factor

Not every customer needs LiDAR, RGB-D, or high-end cascade radar. We ship low-cost, research, vehicle, and high-resolution kits — matched to the use case.

04

Privacy & data policy

RGB and skeleton serve as development-time supervision. When the deployment must be camera-free, the product ships mmWave-only — the same models, no camera.

DopplerMAE encoder

Pre-trained on raw mmWave RA/RD patterns to learn radar-native spatiotemporal motion features — no reliance on hand-crafted point-cloud features. Reusable across HAR, pose, driving, and drone detection.

DopplerVLM foundation model

Built on the DopplerMAE encoder and trained across 80+ hours of radar data on 8×A100. An early radar-language model that converts mmWave streams into activity and scene descriptions, captioning, and Q&A.
